## Custom roles

The three built-in roles (Owner, Admin, Member) aren't your only options anymore. You can now build your own.

### How to make one

1. Open a project from the sidebar.
2. Click the **Roles** tab.
3. Press **Create custom role**.
4. Give it a name. Pick a color and an icon so it's easy to spot.
5. Turn on the things people with this role should be allowed to do.
6. Save.

The new role is ready to use right away. You can give it to anyone on the project, and it shows up next to their name with the color and icon you picked.

### What you can turn on or off

- Who can **upload files**
- Who can **delete files**
- Who can **invite new people**
- Who can **remove people or change their role**
- Who can **edit the project's name and description**
- Who can **create or edit roles themselves**

Each switch is independent, so you can give someone the ability to upload without also giving them the ability to invite people, or any other combination that makes sense for your team.

### A few details

- You can change a custom role at any time. Anyone who has it sees the change within a few seconds — they don't need to reload anything.
- Deleting a custom role moves the people who had it back to the default Member role.
- The built-in Owner, Admin, and Member roles still work exactly as before.

## Remove members from a project

There's now a dedicated way to take someone off a project.

1. Open a project.
2. Find the person in the **Members** list.
3. Press the **Remove** button next to their name.
4. Confirm in the dialog that appears.

They're removed straight away, and everyone else on the project sees it without having to refresh anything.

---

## Live updates for your team

When something changes about who's on a project, everyone sees it right away.

- Someone new joins → they appear in the **Members** list within a second or two.
- Someone leaves → they disappear from the list at the same moment.
- Someone's role is changed → the badge next to their name updates live.
- If you get removed from a project you're viewing, you're sent back to the project picker with a friendly message instead of being left on a page you can no longer access.

No buttons to press, no page to refresh.

## Notifications page, redesigned

Open **Notifications** in the sidebar — the whole page has been rebuilt.

### Tabs for what matters

A strip of tabs at the top lets you focus on what you care about right now:

- **All** — show everything
- **Critical** — failures that need your attention (something didn't go through, the updater hit a problem)
- **High** — things you should do soon (a new app version is ready, an update is waiting to install)
- **Normal** — everyday confirmations (a file was uploaded, a project was created)
- **Low** — quiet things you probably don't need to read (you signed in, you signed out, the app cleared its cache)

Each tab shows a live count, so you can see at a glance whether anything urgent is waiting.

### An icon for every event

Every notification now has a small icon that matches what happened — a bin for things you deleted, an envelope for invitations, an upward arrow for uploads, a sparkle for housekeeping. You can tell at a glance what kind of event a row is without reading the title.

### Clearer times

Anything from the last day says **Today** or **Yesterday**. Anything older shows you the exact date and time. No more guessing what "22 hours ago" actually means.

### Sign-in and app-update events too

Signing in, signing out, profile edits, and app-update events (a new version is downloading, ready to install, or hit a problem) now show up in your notification history alongside everything else. One place to look back at anything the app has told you.

## Theme picker

The app is no longer locked to one look.

1. Open the **Account** page from the sidebar.
2. Find the **Theme** section.
3. Pick the one you like.

The whole app — sidebar, dashboards, dialogs, the Updates page, everything — switches to the new colors immediately. Your choice is remembered, so the app looks the way you left it the next time you open it.

## Faster file previews

Opening a file you've already looked at is now almost instant.

The first time you open a big PDF, it still takes a moment to render. After that, flipping back to it is immediate — no spinner, no waiting. The same goes for the rest of your visit: every file you've already opened reopens in a single beat.

This resets each time you close the app, so memory stays tidy.

Your projects, files, team members, custom roles, and notifications are all safe — nothing about your existing data changes.
